## What's Happening

This piece has four channels working together:

1. **Drums** — `"bd ~ rim sd"` with `.mask("1 1 0 1")` silencing the third beat for a syncopated feel
2. **Hi-hats** — steady quarter notes at 50% volume, nudged 0.01 cycles late for a human feel
3. **Bass** — saw wave playing `0 0 3 5` at half speed (one phrase per 2 cycles), filtered at 700Hz
4. **Melody** — triangle wave descending `7 5 3 0`, shifted 1/8 cycle early to lead the beat

Key techniques:
- `.mask()` selectively silences events using a binary pattern
- `.late()` / `.early()` nudge timing for groove and feel
- `.slow(2)` stretches the bass across two cycles
- `.lpf(700)` keeps the saw wave warm and non-harsh

This pattern is adapted from the **coastline-subset** parity fixture, which is verified to produce equivalent audio across Tussel and Strudel.
