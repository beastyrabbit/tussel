## What's Happening

- `.jux(x => x.fast(2))` plays the pattern in the left channel normally, and a double-speed version in the right channel
- `.room(0.2)` adds subtle reverb to blend the two channels together
- The original 4-event pattern becomes a rich stereo texture
- `jux` ("juxtapose") splits transforms across stereo: one side gets the transform, the other stays untouched
- Related transforms: `off` layers a time-shifted copy, `superimpose` layers a transformed copy at the same time
