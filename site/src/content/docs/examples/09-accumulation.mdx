`jux`, `off`, and `superimpose` create layered variations of a single pattern — stereo widening, delayed echoes, and parallel transforms.

## What's Happening

- `.off(0.125, x => x.gain(0.3))` creates a copy of the pattern shifted 1/8 cycle later, at 30% volume — a rhythmic echo
- `.jux(x => x.fast(2))` plays the pattern in the left channel normally, and a double-speed version in the right channel
- The original 4-event pattern becomes a rich stereo texture with offset echoes
- `off` is like `superimpose` but with a time shift — it layers a modified copy on top of the original
- `jux` ("juxtapose") splits transforms across stereo: one side gets the transform, the other stays untouched
